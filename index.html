About Me

Student Photographer · Leander ISD

Derek Wright

I'm a student photographer with a deep passion for portrait and street-style photography. My work explores human connection, authenticity, and the way light shapes the world around us — shot primarily on a Sony a6400.

Photography found me during my sophomore year and quickly became more than a class — it became a language. I shoot primarily with a Sony a6400 paired with an 18–135mm lens, which gives me the flexibility to move between tight environmental portraits and wider street scenes without missing a beat.

My sustained investigation centred on isolation and its effects on creativity — a theme that pushed me to experiment with negative space, minimal compositions, and the quiet tension that exists when a subject stands alone in a frame. That project changed how I see every shot I take.

Beyond digital, I've explored the tactile side of photography through cyanotype printing and traditional darkroom work. There's something irreplaceable about the chemistry of an analog print — the grain, the unpredictability, the physicality of the image. Both worlds inform each other in my work.

I'm currently in Photography II, continuing to refine my eye and push my technical skills. My goal is to eventually shoot editorial and documentary-style portraiture, telling the stories of people and places through genuine, unposed moments.
